Beef Back Ribs

Image source: Eat Simple Food blog

Description

Basically, covered, 275 °F, 4 hours but you can go up to 5 hr. 6 is probably fine too. Can turn the heat down to, say, 200 °F after 4 hours as a good option.

Some recipes indicate that you should turn the ribs upside down in the pan for cooking. I've tried this and as far as I can tell, it's a wash. I typically place them rightside up because with the ribs curving upward, less of the meat becomes submerged in the rendered fat during cooking.

Ingredients

  • Beef Back Ribs, 2 racks (~5 lb)

Tools

  • 2 large, shallow baking pans
  • Aluminum foil
  • Potholder

Steps

  1. Lay the ribs rightside up (or upside down if you prefer), one rack of ribs on each tray.
  2. Cover the ribs with foil.
  3. Place both pans into the oven. Set the temperature to 275 degF.
  4. You can check on the ribs after 4 hr. The ribs in the pan that you placed on a higher up rack in the oven will cook more slowly.
